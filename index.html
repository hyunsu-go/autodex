AutoDex

Autonomous real-world data collection for dexterous grasping.

AutoDex closes the loop between simulation-generated grasp candidates and real-world validation: it executes multi-finger grasps, labels lift-and-hold success or failure, and actively resets objects to collect physically grounded dexterous-grasp data with far less manual effort than teleoperation.

Abstract

Real dexterous-grasp data is expensive: simulation is scalable but unreliable for contact, while human teleoperation is physically valid but slow and biased by operator choices. AutoDex is an automated real-world data-collection system that proposes geometrically feasible grasps in simulation and validates each one by physically executing it with a multi-fingered hand.

The system automates the collection loop: it accepts candidates from a generator, localizes the object with dense 20-camera tracking, plans and executes collision-monitored motions, labels lift-and-hold success or failure, and actively resets the object for the next trial — then repeats. Using AutoDex we collect 3,593 real-world-validated grasps (successes and failures) across the Allegro and Inspire hands on 100 objects, 4.8× faster than teleoperation; real-world validation raises downstream grasp success from 34% to 76%.

The cycle behind every grasp

Every trial runs these four steps automatically — then the loop closes and starts the next grasp. Click a step.

1 Perceive
2 Plan & grasp
3 Lift & label
4 Reset  ↻

1 · Perceive

A dense 20-camera rig localizes the object — even under severe hand–object occlusion — and reconstructs the surrounding scene, so the system knows exactly what it is grasping and where.

2 · Plan & grasp

A candidate grasp is selected and a collision-free arm trajectory is planned to it. The hand closes on the object — with torque monitoring guarding every contact during automated runs.

3 · Lift & label

The robot lifts and holds. Lift-and-hold success or failure is measured automatically and written as a ground-truth label — the dataset records both the wins and the misses.

4 · Reset ↻

The object is rarely where the next trial needs it. AutoDex re-grasps and re-poses it to the next target pose automatically — the step that keeps the loop going. Then it begins again.
