AutoDex: An Automated Real-World System
for Dexterous Grasping Data Collection

Collecting real-world-validated dexterous grasps — successes and failures — with no human in the per-trial loop.

AutoDex runs unattended. Candidate grasps are generated in simulation, validated on a real multi-fingered hand under a 20-camera capture rig, and the workspace is reset autonomously between trials.

Abstract

Dexterous grasping requires real-world data, yet existing collection paradigms force a choice between simulation (cheap but contact-unreliable) and human teleoperation (faithful but unscalable). We argue that the underlying obstacle is a structural property of the problem: sim-to-real transfer for grasping is asymmetric — geometric feasibility (IK solvability, collision avoidance, kinematic reachability) transfers reliably from simulation, while contact dynamics (friction, slip, multi-finger force closure) do not.

We present AutoDex, a system that operationalizes this asymmetry. Candidate grasps are generated cheaply in simulation and pre-filtered under deliberately optimistic settings; surviving candidates are validated on a real multi-fingered hand by an automated execution pipeline (BODex generation, virtual-obstacle scene synthesis, CuRobo motion planning, dense 20-camera tracking, autonomous reset). A coverage-preserving selection algorithm allocates the limited real-execution budget to maximize scenario solvability across constrained scenes (walls, shelves, boxes). The system is generator-agnostic and runs unattended, producing AutoDex-51, a real-world-validated dataset of 1,678 dexterous-grasp trajectories with both successes and failures. Real-filtered grasps achieve 71.0% success across constrained scenes versus 33.0% for sim-validated grasps, and coverage-preserving selection beats random sampling at every grasp budget.

Why real-world validation matters

Many dominant dexterous failure modes — finger-pad deformation, micro-slip, force closure across simultaneous contacts — are not parameters any simulator exposes. Validating on real hardware more than doubles downstream success across constrained scenes.

System Overview

Given a candidate generator and a multi-fingered hand, AutoDex automatically executes each candidate, observes its outcome, and resets the workspace — producing ground-truth physical labels with no teleoperator and no per-trial supervision.

The Pipeline

An end-to-end autonomous loop, running at about 150 s per sample.

1Generation & pre-filter

Candidate grasps are generated in simulation (BODex) inside virtual-obstacle scenes and pre-filtered under deliberately optimistic settings to keep geometrically feasible candidates.

2Coverage-preserving selection

A max-coverage selection algorithm allocates the limited real-execution budget to maximize scenario solvability across constrained scenes.

3Real-world execution

CuRobo plans collision-free motion; a dense 20-camera synchronized RGB rig tracks the object and hand throughout each lift-and-hold trial.

4Autonomous reset

A hardware-aware sequential reset restores the workspace between trials, letting the system run unattended and collect both successes and failures.

The AutoDex-51 Dataset

Real-world-validated dexterous-grasp trajectories — successes and failures — captured by a 20-view synchronized RGB rig across four scene types (tabletop, wall, shelf, box).
